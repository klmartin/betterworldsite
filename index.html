About Us

At Better World Charity, we believe that every person deserves dignity, compassion, and opportunity—regardless of their circumstances. Founded to address the struggles faced by the most vulnerable in our communities, we are committed to supporting elders, widows, orphans, street children, and the sick.

Our work is rooted in compassion and action. We provide access to education, healthcare, skill development, and emotional support, while also advocating for inclusive policies that protect and empower marginalized groups.

Guided by our vision of a society where no one is left behind, we strive to build a community where hope replaces despair, and where every individual can live a meaningful life filled with respect and opportunity.

Mission

To provide compassionate support and empowerment to elders, orphans, widows, the sick, and street children, fostering a world where everyone has the opportunity for a dignified life, regardless of their circumstances.

Vission

Compassionate and inclusive world where elders, the sick ,street children ,ophans and widows are supported, empowered and integrated into society with dignity and respect.

Objectives

Main Objectives

  • Enhance the well-being and quality of life of the elderly, orphans, widows, the sick, and street children through care, empowerment, and advocacy initiatives.

Specific Objectives

  • Ensure safe living conditions for vulnerable groups.
  • Provide educational opportunities for children and youth.
  • Offer emotional support for holistic development and social integration.
  • Facilitate access to healthcare services.
  • Promote skill development and employment opportunities.
  • Advocate for the rights and needs of marginalized communities.
  • Raise awareness to foster inclusive and compassionate societies.

Frequently Asked Questions

These are some frequently asked questions, If you have any question(s), Do not hestate to question us!.

1. Is Better World Charity a registered organization?

Yes. Better World Charity is a legally registered Non-Governmental Organization under the Non-Governmental Organizations Act, No. 24 of 2002. We are authorized to operate nationally across Tanzania.

2. Does the organization have a vision and mission?

Yes. Our vision is to create a compassionate and inclusive world where elders, orphans, widows, the sick, and street children are supported and empowered. Our mission focuses on care, empowerment, and advocacy to improve quality of life.

3. How are beneficiaries registered?

Beneficiaries are identified through community outreach programs, local leaders, and partnerships with social workers. Once assessed, they are enrolled to receive support and services tailored to their specific needs.

4. Is there an age limit to become a beneficiary?

No. Our programs are designed to support individuals of all ages, from children and youth to widows and the elderly, depending on their vulnerability and needs.

5. Does the organization engage in other social work?

Yes. Beyond direct support, we participate in awareness campaigns, advocacy, and community empowerment initiatives to promote dignity, equality, and social integration for marginalized groups.
